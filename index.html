Veda Duddu

MS CS · UIUC

Please feel free to get in touch with me!

I'm an HCI researcher building methodology around a simple but underexplored question: how do people understand the systems they use, and how should those systems be designed with that understanding in mind?

I came to CS through mathematics at Ashoka University in India, where I graduated with a BS (Honors) in Math and CS. I tend to think about computing problems through a cognitive and social lens, not just a technical one. I currently TA for CS 173: Discrete Mathematics at UIUC.

Outside the lab, I'm a polyglot (English, Hindi, Kannada, Telugu — and slowly, Japanese), follow tennis and cricket with more investment than is probably reasonable, and read across more genres and formats than I can keep track of. I'm also interested in philosophy of mind, the ethics of technology, and occasional deep dives into topics that have nothing to do with my work but somehow always connect back to it. The blog is where those threads live.
